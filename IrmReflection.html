Reflections

Unit 1

This unit exposed me to various definitions of risk management key concepts
and principles that governs information security management and also learning and discussion
around various standards and approach to quantify and qualify risk.
It was particularly interesting to find out how important the type of approach adopted to measure
and communicate security metrics to organisation. It was interesting meeting up with the group and been
able to reach conclusion on the channels for further meetings, drawing up contracts and allocating task accordingly.
I am particularly looking forward to this module as I haven’t done anything academically on risk before
and transitioning through this unit gives me a very good start.

Unit 2

Progressing from unit1, there were further discussion around assessment approaches.
Emphasis was laid on the differences between the approaches as well as highlighting the
importance of and the changes brought by user participation in the process of managing risk.
The research by Spears & Barki (2010) provided a great insight on the implementation
of qualitative and quantitative assessment.
However, capturing how lack of user access affects risk assessment and how and the issues
encountered can be mitigated in 300 words as part of my assessment for a bit challenging.
Again, meeting with group on various occasion across the unit was great as we were able to
review and reflect on the pieces, we are working relating to status report.

Unit 3

I am very well in tuned with the process and methodologies used to implement SDLC,
however the knowledge around how risk affects each phase of SDLC is one I was looking
forward to adding to my toolkit hence my excitement about this unit.
Exposure on Devops pipeline as a means of testing each release for risk and security
issues was great as this directly applicable to my work been a quality engineer.
There were quite challenging moment with time in this unit. The status report was due for
submission and various meeting were held across the team to consolidate review and reflect on all the work done.
Also find time to listen to recording of the seminar from week 2

Unit 4

This unit provides me with opportunity to deep dive into the discussion around common risk in
software development life circle and the best way to mitigate them.
One thing to hold on to going forward is that risk mitigation has the ultimate capacity
to make or break or determine the success software development life circle.
It was quite interesting researching and reading articles and having a discussion around
best mitigation possible for a solution both on team and individual level.
Justifying a chosen mitigation could be challenging as couple of articles needs to be read to surface your argument.
Again, couldn’t make the seminar as there are challenges around the timings.
Various meetings was held within the group across the unit as usual and started working on the team project

Unit 5

It was quite interesting reading around business continuity BC and having an idea
on how to set up a plan to deal with difficulty going forward.
It was paramount to have this disaster recovery and able to explore all the benefits it offers to business globally.
Also, this was directly and practically applicable to the ongoing project on a group level.
Concluding can be a bit challenging as there are different views and opinions from a different author across the globe.
However, reviews critical analysis and collaboration with team has helped in understanding the full concept of business recovery.
It appeared as if there were a bit of stretch in this unit considering the dept in group work and various articles to catch up with.
However, it was particularly interesting for me with respect to the exposure I have on business continuity.
In between I was able to listen to the recording of the previous week seminar.

Unit 6

This unit provides me with opportunity to explore, identify, and analyse trends that could determine the future of information risk management.
