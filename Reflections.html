Reflections

Unit 1

In this unit learning was centred around a brief history of information security management and some important principle that governs the concept of information security management.
There were discussion around cyber security roles and part ways to a fulfilling career in information security management industry though specific roles wasn’t clear at this stage,
but the exposure on this was very helpful as it puts on the table some visibility on areas to explore when it comes to career role.
This unit was particularly important as it forms some basics for the whole module.
Also, there was great awareness on how the whole module was structured going forward.
However, meeting with the group wasn’t great at this stage as we struggled with time differences across Zone.
Overall, it was great been able to develop a contract and have our team goal mapped out irrespective of this challenge.

Unit 2

Following from unit 1, unit 2 offers me the opportunity to learn and explore the real challenges surrounding security.
Using a case study of a medical mannequin there was an opportunity to review, collaborate, contribute and share knowledge with pears
on how real world issues connected with the vulnerabilities of, and threats towards, medical implants.
This case study was helpful in the sense that it provided an insight not only how test are been conducted but also how threats can be mitigated against.
However, I was unable to attend the unit 2 seminar due to a clash in schedules but was able to listen to recordings which was
instrumental in having the required insight on Threat Modeling using DREAD and STRIDE approach for analysing the security of an application

Unit 3

This unit provides a brief history on the emergence of computer networking with an insight into network fundamentals,
it’s concept, the architecture by which networking tools are designed and more importantly how to ensure security is in place for networks.
I was able to summarise the inputs from the initial post and from other pears which emphasises the need to implement security
at development the stage with knowledge of vulnerabilities in mind to protect medical devices.

Unit 4

This unit presents me with the opportunity to perform some practical activity like basic troubleshooting and investigation
using trace route which is a command line utility that identifies the exact path a data packet takes as it travels across the internet.
Interestingly, it was good to know how tools like WINMTR and nslookup helps with further analysis and reports generation.
This very important when it comes to advising management, setting policies and making business decisions
Collaboration from team was great as we are able to compare and dissect differences from our scans.

Unit 5

This unit provides me the opportunity to explore and analyse different network tools and their security implications.
It was paramount to have that exposure on cloud virtualisation and able to explore all the benefits it offers to business globally.
Also, I was able to review, critically analyse and contribute to other peers scan result following from unit from unit 4

Unit 6

This unit exposes me to various tools that can be used for vulnerability scanning
It was quite exciting to know quite a few numbers of tools and be able to evaluate and perform some basic assessment.
Though there wasn’t enough time for some detailed hands-on practises however, creating time for this will provide
provide some deep dive knowledge on which tools best suits organisation needs.
Also, collaborated with the team in a joint review of the design document and provided some recommendation based on the Vulnerabilities identified.

Unit 7

This unit introduced me to the concept of risk as a process of assessment and security standards as an aspect of information security management.
I was also exposed to best practices and technologies for mitigating cyber risk.
Getting familiarised with General Data Protections Regulation was important as this sets the standards that guarantees a consistency of
approach and terminology hence the need to dig in on this to ensure proper implementations going forward.

Unit 8

This unit was quite interesting as it offers the opportunity to gain in-dept understanding of various GDPR rules as it relates to the case study reviewed.
I have a good understanding of security compliance best practices and why they are important
thereby leaving me in a good position where I can advise an organisation on mitigation and meeting set standards
Joining the seminar this unit was challenging as usual due to clash in schedule.
However, it was great listening to the recordings and getting other people’s view and understanding
of the security compliance management and it’s implementation in a world where the dynamic nature of threat and vulnerabilities remain constant.

Unit 9

As a Quality engineer, I have some basic knowledge and exposure on logging tools but the knowledge on Forensics was entirely new and I was looking forward to it.
I learned and familiarised with different types of logs and tools used. Also, I explored how logging is used in different incident investigation.
Reading and learning about forensic tools and techniques was very interesting however, I must admit that at the end of this unit
I feel there still exist a gap on the full context on how to determine the best practices around forensic in terms of techniques to use and I will work on this going forward.

Unit 10

This unit exposes the power of check list when it comes to responding, analysing and reporting a breach which will not
only help to keep track of progress but more importantly also helps prioritise it.
Also, I was able to critically examine and analyse peer contribution on the security breach highlighted on
on my initial post in week 8 and made a summary based those comments and suggestion.

Unit 11

This unit dived more into the future of security. I learned about the emerging trend and technologies with
respect to the awareness around the dynamic nature of threats and how to overcome this challenge.
I also learned about all the facets of network transformation up on till network virtualisation and all the benefits associated with it.

Unit 12

This was very exciting unit as it caps the bits and bobs of what the entire module was involved with.
There were learning around the internet architecture and future potentials associated with it.
